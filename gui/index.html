First, a quick checkup.

RawConvert shrinks RAW photos to a fraction of their size. Before anything else, let's make sure this Mac has the tools it needs. Nothing is ever deleted by this app — you stay in charge of that, always.

Which photos?

Pick the folder of RAW photos (subfolders are included). We'll only look for now — nothing changes yet.

See it with your own eyes.

We'll convert one photo to every format and open the results in Preview so you can zoom in and judge the quality yourself. Sizes show up here.

How should they be kept?

DNG is the recommended choice: it stays an editable RAW file and, on modern cameras, ends up smaller than a JPEG.
